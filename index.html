A little about me...

I am a UX Designer and researcher with diverse skills ranging from physical product design and front-end web developement. Based in Seattle, Washington, I graduated with a degree in Human Centered Design and Engineering from the University of Washington, where I immersed myself in the world of UX Design and Research.

As an Iranian immigrant, I deeply value and understand the significance of Diversity, Equity, and Inclusion both within a workplace and within the design process. I strongly believe that diverse perspectives and experiences enrich the design solution we create and enable us to build inclusive products that cater to a wide range of users.

My journey into the field of User Experience began in the medical field. Witnessing the struggles faced by immigrant communities in understanding their healthcare providers when translators were unavailable, I was motivated to seek answers to the question: "How can the hospital system, medical applications, or hospital devices be redesigned to benefit everyone, regardless of what language they speak?". This experience ignited my passion for UX and set the foundation for my design philosophy.

Throughout my diverse professional experience in UX, I've emphasized the importance of inclusive design as a guiding principle in all my projects. I strive to create designs that meet the needs of diverse users, ensuring accessibility and benefits for everyone, regardless of their background. With a deep understanding of UX design principles, attention to detail, and a passion for meaningful user-experience, I'm dedicated to crafting innovative solutions that become the liason for the gap between technology and people. Through collaboration with multidisciplinary teams and user-centered research methods, my goal is to deliver intuitive and impactful designs that positively transform users' lives. Let's work together to create user-centric and inclusive experiences that leave a lasting impact.
